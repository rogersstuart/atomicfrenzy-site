Our Research Focus

Atomic Frenzy undertakes carefully designed experiments to examine how small‑molecule compounds behave under different conditions. Our goal is to provide actionable data on the formulation and stability of these compounds without divulging proprietary information or specific formulations. Our current initiatives include:

  • Dissolution profiling – evaluating the dissolution characteristics of compounds in various solvent systems. Understanding solubility and dissolution rates is essential for predicting how compounds will perform in different environments.
  • Stability assessments – assessing compound stability under varying storage conditions. We monitor changes in physical and chemical properties over time to determine optimal storage strategies.
  • Solubility and precipitation analysis – characterizing solubility limits, precipitation tendencies and degradation behaviour. This work helps ensure compounds remain suitable for their intended research applications.

Our research is non‑clinical and non‑diagnostic. We conduct these studies under controlled laboratory conditions with qualified personnel and do not release data that could be used for therapeutic or diagnostic purposes.

Ongoing Projects

Beyond our core research activities, Atomic Frenzy pursues forward‑looking projects designed to push the boundaries of formulation science. These initiatives explore new methodologies, materials and analytical approaches to improve the stability and performance of small‑molecule compounds. Examples include:

  • Green solvent systems – designing environmentally friendly solvent mixtures to enhance dissolution profiles and reduce the use of hazardous chemicals.
  • Predictive stability modelling – developing computational models that predict long‑term stability and degradation pathways using machine learning and accelerated stability data.
  • Microencapsulation and controlled release – investigating novel microencapsulation techniques and polymer carriers to control solubility and release rates of research compounds.
  • Advanced polymer carriers – engineering custom polymer matrices to improve the solubility and stability of poorly soluble compounds under varied environmental conditions.
